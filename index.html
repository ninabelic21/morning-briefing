🔦 Spotlight: Liberation Day — One Year Later

The biggest market story this week: the anniversary of Trump's sweeping tariffs

One year ago on April 2, 2025, President Trump announced "Liberation Day" tariffs — sweeping duties including 34% on China, 20% on the EU, and 46% on Vietnam. The initial shock sent the US stock market plunging 12.4%, the biggest drop since COVID-19. Markets then surged 9.52% in a single day when Trump paused parts of the policy — the largest one-day S&P 500 gain since 2008.

Fast forward to today: the S&P 500 is up 16% from Liberation Day, but US stocks have actually underperformed the global index (MSCI ACWI +18% vs MSCI USA +14%). The Supreme Court ruled the tariff regime unconstitutional, and the government may owe billions in refunds from $166 billion collected from 330,000+ businesses.

The anniversary has reignited debate about "American exceptionalism" in investing. Global investors are increasingly diversifying away from US-centric portfolios, and the stronger performance of international indices suggests the tariffs may have permanently shifted capital flows.
